# critters v0.3 — "make it cool" design (2026-07-06, S60 round 2)

Trent-approved scope, all five items plus two riders:

- RIDER 1: every new 3D element (trees, rocks, all of it) uses the SDF blend-shell
  technique. No plain meshes in the world.
- RIDER 2: harness grows a multi-angle visual inspector (move camera, focus subjects,
  shoot from many angles) because visible artifacts remain: occasional clipping
  weirdness, "vertex bounds explosions" (shrapnel spikes), faces set too deep inward
  (creases at prim overlaps, e.g. crimson quad neck). Fix the defaults BEFORE adding
  content. Claude should "see" while working.

## 0. Quality pass (first, blocking)

- `CRITTERS.view(i, theta, dist, h)` debug API: place camera at azimuth theta around
  critter i; `tools/inspect.mjs` shoots N angles x close/far into `verify/inspect/`.
- Root causes identified (index.html VSH main):
  - Inward creases: `bury` smoothstep(0.55, 1.15) sends overlap verts to negative iso
    (-rT*0.4); triangles spanning buried+surface verts cut through the skin. Soften:
    bury later (deeper threshold) and tuck shallower.
  - Vertex explosions: Newton steps `p -= g*(d-iso)` unclamped; bad gradients in smin
    valleys launch verts. Clamp per-step travel to ~1.2*rT.
  - Clipping: fixed boundingSphere 4*S too tight for serpent chain (up to ~3.3 behind
    pos) and long ropes; frustum pops at screen edge. Scale bounds per archetype.
- Verify: before/after inspect sheets on same seeds; featured cast must look identical
  or better from 6 angles.

## A. Meadow (SDF blend-shell flora)

- StaticShell builder: same prim/shader pipeline as Critter but built once; only trees
  get per-frame endpoint writes (canopy sway = slow sine on canopy blob prims + trunk
  lean; no verlet needed). Rocks/mushrooms/flowers fully static (skip per-frame writes).
- Props: tree (trunk round-cone + 2-4 canopy blobs), rock clusters (2-3 gray-green
  blobs), mushroom (stem + cap + spots), flower tuft (thin stem prims + colored head).
- Seeded scatter ring outside the play area (r 6..14), sparse inside (avoid spawn zone);
  deterministic from scene seed; rerolls re-scatter.
- Perf budget: <= 10 props visible, each one draw call pair (body+outline share the
  cached program from the perf fix; uCount handles size). Keep total prims/frame sane.

## B. Owl archetype

- Perched bird: sits on tree canopy or ground stumps; big blinky eyes (reuse eye system,
  large r), head swivel (headYaw sweeps with occasional fast snap, beyond normal clamp),
  tiny wing nub prims. Occasionally glides (flyer-style bank) to another perch.
- Key 7 + UI button. genRecipe branch. Featured "angry owl" easter egg recipe (nod to
  clayafterdark in the thread).

## C. Name-to-critter

- `#n=<text>` URL param + input field inside the credits panel ("make YOUR critter"):
  string -> 32-bit hash (mulberry32 seed) -> genRecipe with deterministic archetype
  pick. Spawns front-and-center with a toast showing the name. Share button includes
  the name when the nearest critter came from one.

## D. Ambient layer

- Procedural audio bed: filtered-noise wind (slow LFO gain) + sparse random birdsong
  chirps (existing synth voice, higher register). Obeys the same mute toggle.
- Cloud shadows: 2-3 large soft dark sprites drifting slowly across the ground
  (reuse ground-patch look) + slightly warmer horizon gradient (simpleclouds lineage).

## E. Ecology behaviors (EXPANDED 2026-07-07: "they need to play more autonomously")

Critters should be fun to WATCH with zero input, a little sim you leave running:
- Play behaviors on idle timers: two critters play tag (chaser swaps on touch + happy
  squash), hoppers spontaneously race between two props, flyers orbit tree canopies,
  serpents follow each other head-to-tail (conga), critters gather around a mushroom
  and look at it, solo critters nap (slow squash breathing, closed eyes), wake with a
  jiggle. Weight so 1-2 vignettes run at a time, not chaos.
- Curiosity: petted or possessed critters attract 1-2 curious onlookers that trail at
  a distance; a dashing player scatters bystanders (ties into F5 soft forces).

- Steering additions to wander(): loose flock cohesion (drift toward centroid of
  same-archetype neighbors), flee burst when the possessed critter charges at them,
  curious follow (nearest 1-2 critters trail the possessed player at a distance).
- Babies: 20% recipe roll spawns a 0.45x mini-me that follows its parent (path-follow
  at distance, like serpent segments but a separate critter with the same palette).

## Inspo: Andre's Car Runner 3D (venturous.se/c3d_car, Trent's friend, 2026-07-07)

Verified by playing it headless. Steal list: HUD chips (black pill + huge bold numerals
+ icon; adopt for F12 seed/count/player cards), constant exhaust-puff motion feedback
(critters emit pace puffs while running), landmark-scale framing props (buildings ring
the road; our world wants big anchor props per chunk), collectible counter loop
(kid-safe berries/sparkles as a wander goal + friendly co-op race metric).

## G. Rendering tricks shelf (Trent 2026-07-07: "performant but also cute and cool")

Cheap, mobile-safe, in the SDF-shell language. Pick per polish round, never all at once:
- **Dash ghost shells**: during a dash, re-draw the outline shell 2-3 times at trailing
  positions with fading alpha (pure uniform changes, same cached program) = speed lines
  that look native to the toon shell. THE on-brand trick, do this one first.
- **Trick confetti**: tiny colored sprite burst on B/X tricks (dust system recolored).
- **Footprints**: soft fading dark decal sprites where feet plant, 2s life, pooled.
- **Golden hour**: slow sky/sun tint cycle + warmer rim light at "evening" (uniform
  lerps only), pairs with D ambience. Long soft blob shadows at low sun.
- **Eye sparkles**: star glint sprite on pet/trick moments.
- **Paper grain**: one static fullscreen overlay texture at ~4% opacity, watercolor vibe.
- **Distance LOD**: far critters skip rope sim + eye solve every other frame (logic LOD
  before mesh LOD; meshes are already radius-scaled).

## F. Playtest backlog (Trent, live session 2026-07-06 evening, controller in hand)

Confirmed wins: reroll speed felt immediately ("kicked balls"), pad play is wicked,
antenna bounce is GREAT. New asks, his words distilled:

- F1 **WASD possession**: keyboard gets the gamepad treatment. WASD camera-relative run
  (auto-possess nearest on first press), Space jump, Tab cycles critters, Esc releases.
- F2 **Eyes buried, SYSTEMIC** (Trent screenshot 2026-07-06: pink multiped shows NO
  eyes, serpent eyes swallowed): eyes are placed on the head-alone surface at build,
  but wherever prims blend (spider body+legs+antennae, serpent head+chain) the union
  surface bulges past them. Fix: project eye positions onto the LIVE union surface
  (per-frame or per-recipe CPU Newton step along the field gradient) so eyes always
  sit ON the skin. Verify across many seeds x archetypes with the F3 sweep.
- F8 **Eye animation pass**: darting saccades (quick pupil jumps with holds, not just
  smooth lerp), look-at IK feel, occasional double-blink, pupils that scan the ground
  while wandering. "all eyes should have more animation, look darting, the full sim
  experience"
- F3 **Permutation sweeps**: harness runs many seeds x archetypes with close-up
  multi-angle shots, so eye placement / welding / outline bugs surface without a human
  spotting them. ("harness test more permutations man!")
- F4 **More jiggle**: ropes only visibly bounce while moving. Add idle micro-jiggle
  everywhere: breathing already exists, add subtle rope excitation at idle, tiny body
  wobble. "everything jiggle a little more thatd be cool"
- F5 **Soft collision reactions**: NOT damage, soft forces. Keep the gentle separation
  push, add reaction juice: squash pulse + velocity nudge + occasional dust when
  critters bump, bigger reaction when the possessed critter plows through.
  CRITICAL BUG (Trent screenshot 2026-07-07, firefox_mDeTJ6Bp0c): a possessed critter
  can fully EMBED inside another and stay there. separate()'s push is (overlap*1.6*dt),
  which loses to drive speed. Fix: push strength ramps with overlap depth (quadratic or
  ~6x when overlap > 40%), and make it asymmetric KNOCKBACK: the moving/possessed
  critter transfers momentum, the victim gets shoved with a squash pulse and skid dust;
  the driver barely slows ("more knockback and stuff"). Two idle critters still just
  drift apart softly. Verify by driving a possessed critter into a standing one:
  sustained overlap must be impossible.
- F6 **Remove the invisible wall, use chunking**: world opens up. Ground becomes chunks
  streamed around the camera/possessed critter; groundY is already procedural. Critter
  wander recenters near the player/camera instead of clamping to r=9. Props scatter
  per chunk, despawn with them.
- F7 **More animals** (asked twice = priority): new archetypes/variants beyond owl.
  Candidates: crab (sideways strafe gait), snail (shell + slow slime), butterfly/bee
  (flyer variants), long-neck quadruped variant, chunky bear variant.
- F11 **Face buttons per creature** (Trent 2026-07-07): every face button does something
  different per archetype. A = jump/dash (shipped). B = movement trick: walkers wet-dog

- F17 **Physics objects** (Trent 2026-07-07 "rocks should be pushable"): light dynamic

- F18 **Village direction, "turn it into Animal Crossing"** (Trent 2026-07-07): placeable
